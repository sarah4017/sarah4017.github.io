My Crush on C/C++


Born and raised in Ontario, I moved to Calgary to study at the University of Calgary and run for the Dinos Cross-Country team in 2016.

As an athlete, I was fortunate to win the 2016 Canada West Cross-Country Rookie of the Year award and become a member of the team that went on to win the 2018 and 2019 USPORTS National Championships.

My first experience programming was in a first year course, ENGG 233 - Computing for Engineers. Since then, I had my first work experience in software development at the Calgary Counselling Centre in the summer of 2019, and began a 16-month internship at Lockheed Martin CDL Systems in May 2020.

I have used different languages over the last few years, but lately my interests have shifted more towards C/C++. I like the idea of systems that have physical devices in the real world that communicate data back to software systems. Working with control station software for unmanned systems is exactly that, so I really enjoyed that aspect of my internship with Lockheed Martin.

The next project that I have on my mind to is to build my own running watch. I think this will be quite a challenge, because I have no experience 3D printing (for the watch casing) or designing custom PCBs. It should be an interesting learning opportunity for those skills, as well as an opportunity to revisit some of the topics covered in the "ENCM 511 - Embedded Systems Interfacing". Compared to the other topics covered in school, I feel like the exposure to embedded systems is lacking in the U of C Software Engineering circiculum. Hopefully this project will help to fill that gap in my knowledge.
